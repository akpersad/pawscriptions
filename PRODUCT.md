# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Two people in one household sharing the care of a single dog (a tri-color mini
Australian Shepherd). There are no per-user accounts — they share one dataset behind one
passphrase. They reach for the app in short, real-world moments: at the food bowl in the
morning, last thing before bed, or when a reminder buzzes. Often one-handed, on a phone,
sometimes half-asleep. The job is never "explore the app" — it's "did the dog get the
8am pill, and is anything still due?" answered in under five seconds.

## Product Purpose
Make sure a dog never misses a dose and never gets double-dosed when two caretakers share
the duty. Define medications and schedules once, then log doses with a single tap, see at a
glance what's still due today, and trust a complete audit trail of who gave what and when.
Push reminders close the loop. Success is the app being so fast and legible that checking it
becomes reflexive — and the dog's health record being something you could hand a vet.

## Brand Personality
Warm, caring, and quietly premium — a high-end pet-wellness brand, not a clinical EMR.
Three words: **devoted, dependable, gentle.** The voice is plain and reassuring, never
cute-to-a-fault and never sterile. It should feel like it was made by people who love this
specific dog. Calm confidence over alarm: even "a dose is overdue" is delivered kindly.

## Anti-references
- **Generic medical/EHR software** — gray, dense, anxiety-inducing, form-first.
- **Childish pet apps** — cartoon mascots, Comic Sans energy, rainbow gradients, emoji soup.
- **The 2026 AI-app look** — purple/indigo gradients, glassmorphism everywhere, identical
  icon+heading+text card grids, tiny all-caps tracked eyebrows above every section.
- **Cold productivity minimalism** — all-gray, characterless, could-be-any-SaaS.

## Design Principles
1. **Answer the one question first.** Every screen leads with the single thing the user
   came for. On Today, that's "what's still due." Hierarchy serves the glance.
2. **One tap to log, zero doubt about state.** Giving a dose, and seeing it's been given,
   must be unmistakable and fast. The shared-care promise lives here: never ambiguous.
3. **Calm, not clinical.** Warmth comes from color, roundness, space, and kind copy — never
   from decoration that slows the task. Reassure; don't alarm.
4. **Earned familiarity.** Standard affordances done impeccably. The tool disappears into
   the task; nothing is reinvented for flavor.
5. **Made for this dog.** A specific, ownable identity (the Aussie's coat) over a stock
   pet-app template. Memorable in the moments that matter, invisible the rest of the time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, in both light and dark themes.
Full keyboard reachability with visible focus rings. Touch targets ≥ 44px (often used
one-handed). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` and `prefers-reduced-transparency`. State is
never conveyed by color alone (icons/text accompany every status). Theme follows the OS
light/dark preference.
